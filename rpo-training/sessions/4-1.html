Session 4.1: Introduction to No-Code Automation

Our Goal: To build a workflow that completely automates a boring task. Let's connect the dots and build a system that works for you, even when you're not looking.

The "Finished Product" Demo

See Where We're Going

Imagine this hands-free workflow we are building today:

  1. An email with candidate feedback arrives in your Gmail and you give it a label.
  2. You do nothing else.
  3. A moment later, a new row appears in your tracking spreadsheet, automatically filled with the candidate's name, a one-sentence summary, and a sentiment score.

How Does Automation Actually Work?

APIs

Think of an API as a waiter in a restaurant. It's how different apps talk to each other, taking requests and returning data.

Triggers

This is the "When this happens..." part of the equation. For us, it's "When a new email gets the label 'Candidate Feedback'..."

Actions

This is the "...Then do that" part. For us, it's "...run my script to analyze it and add it to the spreadsheet."

Our Platform: Google Apps Script

We will be using Google Apps Script. It's built into your Google account and is perfect for connecting Google products.

  • It uses a simplified version of JavaScript.
  • Don't worry! You do not need to be a coder.
  • We will provide you with a well-commented code template. Your job is to understand the logic, not to write code from scratch.

Lab Activity: The End-to-End Workflow (65 Mins)

Your Goal:

To build and test the full, automated workflow from email to spreadsheet.

The Plan of Action:

  1. The Connection: Use the provided template to create a script and set up the trigger to read emails from Gmail.
  2. The AI: Add the Gemini API call to the script to analyze the email's content.
  3. The Destination: Add the final lines of code to populate your Google Sheet.
  4. The Test: Send a sample email to yourself, label it, and watch the magic happen!

The "Magic" Prompt

This is the key to making the workflow intelligent. This prompt forces the AI to give us back perfectly structured data that our spreadsheet can understand.

From the following email text, extract the candidate's name, provide a one-sentence summary of the feedback, and classify the interviewer's sentiment as Positive, Negative, or Neutral. Return this as a JSON object.

Key Takeaways & Next Steps

Your new superpower is that you didn't just run a script; you built a system. The golden rule is to identify the most repetitive, copy-paste parts of your job, as these are the perfect candidates for automation.
