Success Spotlight #1

A Real-World Win from Anna

The Problem: Anna was starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" in a competitive fintech market. The old way involved hours of manual research on LinkedIn to understand the landscape.

The New Way: She built one powerful prompt to do the work for her.

Anna's Winning "Talent Landscape Snapshot" Prompt:

[ROLE] You are a senior market intelligence analyst for a top executive search firm, specializing in the European fintech sector.

[CONTEXT] I am starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" for a new client, a fast-growing fintech startup in Budapest. I need to quickly understand the talent landscape.

[TASK] Create a "Talent Landscape Snapshot" for this role. Identify the top 5 competing companies... list the 2-3 most common job titles... and provide a list of 10-15 key skills...

[FORMAT] Please format the output clearly with headings for "Target Companies," "Common Job Titles," and "Key Skills & Technologies."

The Impact: From Hours to Seconds

  • The AI provided a perfectly formatted report in 30 seconds.
  • It identified target companies and more specific job titles like "VP of Growth."
  • It listed key technologies like "HubSpot" and "Salesforce Marketing Cloud."
  • This saved at least 1 hour of manual research and gave her a huge confidence boost.

Live Prompt Clinic #1

Your Turn to Get Help

The Rule: This is a "no-judgment zone." If you're stuck, chances are someone else is too. We will fix prompts as a group.

Clinic Case Study: Peter's Problem

The Challenge: Peter wanted help writing screening questions for a "Junior Accountant" role, but his results were too generic.

Peter's "Before" Prompt:

Give me interview questions for a junior accountant.

The Group's "After" Prompt:

[ROLE] You are a hiring manager...
[CONTEXT] I need questions for a "Junior Accountant." Key qualifications are attention to detail, Excel pivot tables, and eagerness to learn...
[TASK] Generate a list of interview questions.
[FORMAT] Organize questions into categories: "Attention to Detail," "Excel Skills," and "Learning Agility."

The Result: From Generic to Perfectly Structured

By adding Role, Context, and Format, the AI produced specific, behavioral questions organized into the exact categories needed for a structured screening call. Peter got a ready-to-use list in minutes.
